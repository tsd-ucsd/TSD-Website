About TSD

Background

The goal of what we want to do as a team is address the housing crisis in San Diego, and show that sustainable and affordable housing is very possible. We expect our structure to be roughly 18ft tall with about 1,000 sqft of living space. We are focusing on creating low-cost housing solutions that ate healthy, a joy to inhibit, and are sustainable. To accomplish this, we are taking a holistic approach incorporating cutting-edge research on off-gridding, energy efficiency, solar panels, reflective material to combat heat, and incorporating advanced design and manufacturing to keep costs down. Ultimately, we hope out modular prefabricated microunits can be a beneficial addition to communities like Logan Heights in San Diego. We are working with existing community organizations to see what's needed.

Challenges

As the first ever solar decathlon team at UCSD we have constantly facing new challenges. Although our journey so far has not been the smoothest, we have learned a great deal and we are hoping to implement systems within our organization to pass down what we have learned from each challenge.

Competition

The Orange County Sustainability Decathlon is an international collegiate competition challenging students to integrate high-performance design and inspire the industry through innovative solutions. The UC San Diego team is one of 17 teams competing in the 2023 Sustainability Decathlon at the Orange County Fairgrounds. Our team is working with the Greater Victory Church in the Logan Heights community, Jacobs School of Engineering, Urban Studies and Planning, Resource Management and Planning, Facilities Management, and industry partners.
